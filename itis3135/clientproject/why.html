Mentor button

First of all, become a mentor! Being a mentor on a FIRST team is not only rewarding for the students, but also for the mentors themselves. As mentors, they get to share their passion and knowledge with the next generation of innovators and leaders, as well as learn new skills and perspectives from the students and other mentors. They also get to network with other professionals and volunteers in the STEM community, and experience the excitement and satisfaction of seeing the team grow and succeed. Moreover, they get to inspire and be inspired by the team’s creativity, resilience, and teamwork. You might be wondering, what is a mentor? A mentor is any adult who participates in a FIRST team and leads the students by showing them how to do things and guiding them in their area of expertise. They can be involved for as little or as much time as they can, depending on their availability and the team’s needs. They help the team learn how to approach and solve the game challenge by themselves, without providing them with the ready-made solution. They also act as a coach, a teacher, a motivator, and a facilitator for the team, helping them have a fun and enriching experience. They help the team acquire the skills and confidence to pursue their future goals with FIRST. They also benefit from the learning process and share their enthusiasm for making a positive difference in each other’s lives and in the world. Being a mentor on a FIRST team does not require any special skills or qualifications, but it does require patience, dedication, and a willingness to learn alongside the team. In FIRST, mentors and students are equal partners who collaborate and respect each other in working towards a mutual and beneficial goal. This requires a commitment from everyone involved to make the most of the experience.

Student button

Participating in a FIRST Robotics Competition team is a rewarding and enriching experience for students. It offers them many benefits and opportunities that will help them develop their skills and interests. One of the benefits is working with professional engineers who mentor the students and teach them STEM skills that are essential for their future careers. Another benefit is designing, building, and programming their own robots using advanced software, hardware, and tools that challenge their creativity and problem-solving abilities. A third benefit is improving their teamwork, interpersonal, and communication skills by collaborating with their peers and competing in alliances and tournaments that foster a spirit of sportsmanship and cooperation. A fourth benefit is practicing Gracious Professionalism™, a core value of FIRST that encourages respect and kindness among competitors. They can also qualify for the FIRST Championship, a prestigious event that showcases the best teams from around the world and celebrates their achievements. Finally, a sixth benefit is accessing millions of dollars in college scholarships that are available exclusively for FIRST participants. The impact of FIRST Robotics Competition on students is significant and proven. According to surveys, more than 88% of participants report having more interest in doing well in school and 92% report having more interest in attending college.
